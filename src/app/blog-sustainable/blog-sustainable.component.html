An itinerary for sustainable travel

June 20, 2023 By Avila International

Travelling becomes a beautiful experience only when the places and people we visit are peaceful and thriving. As a traveller, sustainable travel is one way you can ensure you add value to the places you visit!

People who truly love travelling always make a conscious effort to turn it into a fulfilling experience – not only for themselves, but also for the places they visit and people who live there.

Sustainable travel is one way of doing it and surely the need of the hour. Avila is passionate about incorporating sustainable practices into our customised tours and travel, and through this blog, we are going to share with you how you can make your expeditions sustainable and wholesome!

What is sustainable travel?

The United Nations’ World Tourism Organisation (UNWTO) defines sustainable travel as one “that takes full account of its current and future economic, social and environmental impacts, addressing the needs of visitors, the industry, the environment, and host communities.”

In simple words, sustainable travel focuses on reducing the negative impacts of tourism on the environment, while promoting responsible and ethical practices. There are several conscious choices you can make to preserve our planet's natural resources and cultural heritage.

“ 5 ways to make your travels sustainable ”
1. Minimise plastic waste

Eliminate or cut down on single-use plastics while you travel. You can carry reusable water bottles, utensils or cloth shopping bags instead. In case you have to carry or buy plastic items or items packaged in plastic, dispose of them in designated waste/recycle bins.

2. Conserve water and energy

Be mindful of conserving water and energy during your travels. Use water sparingly, and turn off electronics when not in use. You can also choose eco-friendly accommodations or establishments that use renewable energy sources or efficient lighting systems to save energy.

3. Support local communities

Interacting with local people, learning about their culture, and supporting local businesses and artisans not only make travel fun but also responsible. You can support local communities by opting for locally owned accommodations, restaurants, shops, etc. that directly contribute to the local economy.

4 .Respect local wildlife and nature

Include national parks, sanctuaries, and protected areas that promote conservation efforts in your itinerary. Follow the rules and avoid interfering with wildlife. Disengage with activities that exploit animals, such as elephant rides.

5 .Reduce your carbon footprint

Reduce your carbon footprint by opting alternative modes of transport whenever possible – like public transport, cycling or walking for local sightseeing. They’re healthy for you and our planet both, and help you really soak in the experience.

Travelling becomes a beautiful experience only when the places and people we visit are peaceful and thriving. And we can do a lot to facilitate this. If you too love travelling and would want to continue exploring the world, embrace sustainable travelling and inspire those around you to do the same!
